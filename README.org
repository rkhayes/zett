* Zett
** Clarification and Guidance For Your Zettelkasten
These concepts serve as a baseline to help you define and organize your system. They are not rigid rules; ~zett~ is flexible enough to support workflows that depart from these suggestions, so treat this as a reference rather than a mandate.
- *Not everything you write is a Zettel*

  A Zettel is an atomic unit of thought that, when interconnected, forms the backbone of your Zettelkasten. However, not every file in your system qualifies as one.

  Supporting files that do not directly contribute to the "web of ideas" often belong outside your primary slip-box. This is typically the case for meta-files -- documents designed to aggregate or organize existing knowledge rather than generate new insights.

  - Maps of Content (MoCs): While frequently stored within the slip-box for easy access, these are structural nodes rather than atomic ideas.

  - External Projects: A scientific paper or a long-form essay, for instance, has no place inside the slip-box. These are outputs—complex, non-atomic structures that sit at a higher level than the individual notes used to build them.

- *The principle of connectivity*

  A Zettel is only as valuable as its relationships. A note without a link is an "orphan" -- it is effectively lost to your future self because there is no path leading to it.

  This turns your system from an archive (where things tend to pile up and be forgotten) into a living ecossystem where ideas can bloom from existing materia.

- *The collector's fallacy*

  A common pitfall is filling a slip-box with highlights and copy-pasted excerpts. This is "Collector’s Fallacy" -- the belief that having the information is the same as knowing it.

  To cite [[https://x.com/karpathy/status/1756380066580455557][Andrej Karpathy]]:
  #+begin_quote
  Don't just read, take notes, re-read, re-phrase, process, manipulate, learn.
  #+end_quote

  A Zettel should be written in your own words (paraphrased). If you must include a quote, it should be followed by your own commentary or critique.

  You can learn more about this concept [[https://zettelkasten.de/posts/collectors-fallacy/][here]].
